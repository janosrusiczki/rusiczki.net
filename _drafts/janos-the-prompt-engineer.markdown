Is this what it means to be a developer in 2026? I'm an AI coding agent herder telling them what to do and checking their output for hallucinations? Maybe. And I'm not even complaining!

But first a little bit of history about János' interactions with AI.

I'm not sure exactly when I signed up for the Github Copilot trial but I got an email on the 17th of august 2022 that my trial is about to end. The 5th of September was a $0 free plan setup and the first real payment came on the 8th of September. I remember I haven't used it during the trial and I got charged for it which gave me the motivation to start testing it out and while doing that I thought I'd give the competition a chance. So on the 18th of september 2022 I signed up for the Tabnine trial and I somehow forgot to cancel that too. After the 14-day trial ended, on the 2nd of October 2022, I was charged for a $144 yearly subscription. I contacted them that same day and got refunded the next day. And stayed with Copilot since then.

And then came the end of 2022 and the beginning of 2023 when ChatGPT took the world by storm. I remember writing prompts and marveling at the output and soon enough noticing Stack Overflow having a banner which instructed their users that they shouldn't post ChatGPT generated answers. That was the beginning of the end for Stack Overflow, which is a bit unfair since I'm pretty sure ALL of the coding helpers were trained on their content. On the other hand, I remember my frustration not being able to use SO to ask something like: "I have X and Y and want to accomplish Z. What is the best approach? What are the best libraries?" The question would be locked in a short time for being "subjective". So I was very happy as soon as I could pose this questions to LLMs that would just give me an answer.

A bit later in 2023 I signed up and got into the Github Copilot chat beta on the 7th of July. It seemed out of this world: I wasn't merely getting code completions, I could ask natural language questions and the LLM could respond having the context of my code. I didn't even need to copy relevant stuff over into ChatGPT anymore. Wowzers!

Then at the beginning of 2024 Google Gemini (finally) launched and in May 2024 I even got myself Gemini Advanced for a short while, which I used with more or less success to edit [one article here on the blog](https://www.rusiczki.net/2024/09/25/narnia-din-curtea-mea/). It was funny that I only asked it to proof read that article and edit it to avoid repetitions and other writing blunders I'm guilty of but it somehow went off the rails and rewrote half of my article in Spanish. I was amused. The rest of the articles around here are 100% certified AI free though. Pinky promise!

And then came the era of AI coding agents. In May 2025, [Emil](https://www.emilburzo.com) recommended [openrouter](https://openrouter.ai/) with [aider](https://aider.chat/). I used it to successfully add some features to my opensource project and was quite amazed. Then I checked openrouter stats and I saw that Kilo Code appeared a lot.

Then I got hired and the client uses and encourages the use of AI coding assistants. We had some training sessions.

I tried a few things. Installed Kilo Code, wrote rules for it, connected some MCPs.

I topped up my openrouter account with $10

Got a recommendation for z.ai from Andrei but I pretty much ignored it for a while. On the 6th of November 2025, Emil said that I should get myself a Claude Pro account, I did, but it wasn't the wisest choice. 5 hour limit, weekly limit, limits upon limits. Luckily I got a beta test for their web code editor, which gave me $250 to have fun with until November 18 so I tried doing as much as I could.

Also on the 6th of November 2025, I got a hint from Emil for perplexity, that I should associate it with PayPal and I got Perplexity Pro and some sort of Google on steroids. I use perplexity for researching any type of tech acquisition I make. So for example, I researched a projector, graphics cards. I like that it searches the web for current information and actually crafts a response from the findings. I also use it to write copy for websites I have by researching and synthesizing information from different websites.

Then I rediscovered how good Copilot is (it also uses the rules I defined for Kilo Code) and it's also pretty cheap, for huge features it only uses one premium credit.

- I started using it for things that would take me a long time and are not my strong points. Such as UI.
- Tasks that I wouldn't be able to complete in a reasonable amount of time, not having the know how: Related tags. There's no value for me in learning the necessary statistics for one task.
- Boilerplate repetitive tasks that I was procrastinating on
- Writing and adjusting unit tests

- Garbage in garbage out
- Man in the loop
- Rewards for fast feedback which I'm not good at
- It was a welcomed boost for my ADD ass. So many new toys! 

I'm pretty sure the AI bubble will burst, I just hope this doesn't mean that they will take away my toys.
