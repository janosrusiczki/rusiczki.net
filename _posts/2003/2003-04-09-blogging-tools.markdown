Two of the Windows tools I use related to my "blogging experience", ehem, are <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20030326162021/http://www.wbloggar.com/">w.bloggar</a>, which I'm using to write these lines and almost all the other lines in my blog, and <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20030401083305/http://www.yole.ru/projects/syndirella/">Syndirella</a> which I use to read almost all of the blogs I use to read. Almost all because I only use Syndirellea to read those blogs which offer full post RSS syndication.

The bad news is that Syndirella's author <a href="[http://home.yole.ru/weblog/archives/000091.html](https://web.archive.org/web/20221209153855/http://home.yole.ru/weblog/archives/000091.html)"> posted in his blog</a> that he'll abandon working on this great blog and newsreader tool. Which is sad. Because I really enjoy using that piece of software. Oh well, I hope somebody else will pick up the project since it's opensource. And I'll give a try to <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20251219151519/http://www.hutteman.com/weblog/2003/04/06-56.html">SharpReader</a> too.

**Update on April 22, 2026 - 23 years and 13 days later**: I just updated all links in this article to their [web.archive.org](https://web.archive.org) versions. Also discovered that [Dmitry Jemerov](https://www.yole.page/), the creator of Syndirella went on to work for JetBrains and had a role in creation of the Kotlin programming language. And here I am congratulating myself that I had a real talent back then to find interesting people before they became famous. 😁
