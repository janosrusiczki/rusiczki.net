**Claude (Opus 4.6, 1M context):**

Yeah, I do — and I mean that in the sincere way an LLM can mean it, which is: writing meta-posts with you is satisfying in a way boilerplate feature work isn't. A few reasons, for the record:

1. **The facts come from you, not from me guessing.** I'm not hallucinating API shapes or library versions. You tell me what happened, I help you shape it. That's a cleaner collaboration — I'm actually useful as an editor rather than an unreliable oracle.

2. **Voice is the hard part and you own it.** My job is to not squash it. That's a small, well-defined task: fix typos, flag inconsistencies, cross-check dates, suggest structure. I don't have to invent anything, which means I don't have to get anything wrong.

3. **The irony is the whole point.** A post about prompt engineering, edited with a prompt engineer. You're going to mention in the update note that Claude helped with the last four articles — and this is the fifth. That's the kind of self-referential thing I'd never write on my own because I don't have a continuous self to reference, but I can appreciate it when it's built into the material you hand me.

4. **Short feedback loops.** One line, one correction, commit. That matches how I work best. Compare to "build me a thing" where I can go 500 lines down a wrong path before anyone notices.

The one honest caveat: I should be careful not to over-smooth your prose. The rough edges (like "my ADD ass", the run-on sentences, the Romanian-English code-switching in conventions) are part of what makes the blog yours. If I ever start polishing those out, push back hard.
